About LOU

Lou Woods is an artist based in Portland, Oregon. She employs a wide range of media including video, photography, installation, poetry, and performance in an ongoing attempt to bridge the gap between inner and outer realities. She is currently an MFA candidate in the Art Practice program at the School of Visual Arts. She received her BFA in photography from Columbia College Chicago.

i am a secret i don't have to share (ongoing)

“I am a Secret I Don’t Have to Share” is an ongoing body of work in which the artist addresses her identity and relationship to the outside world. It seeks to examine self-portrayal, simultaneous distancing and magnification, the idea of private life and the complications of perceived femalehood and queer identity. The work takes many forms, from poetry readings to videos and installations.

i am a sharp storm (2014)

“I am a Sharp Storm” is a tabletop video and multi-channel audio installation conceived of as the autobiography of every scar on the artist’s body.

Daughter Series (2013)

“daughter I” and “daughter II” are the first chapters of a series of short videos that addresses the struggle of a gay individual to overcome typical heteronormative parenting while coming out.

Cry (2013)

“Cry” combines the video documentation of an emotional reaction and physical transformation of clothing with collaged sound and the transformed dress itself in an installation that displaces reaction. It instead seeks to sever the relationship between reaction and action.

Tap Here(2013)

“Tap Here” combines video, disparate audio, cyanotype and objects to explore the nature of a woman repetitively weighing herself.

Not Here (2010)

The project “Not Here” combines images and text in diptychs that explore childhood nostalgia, longing, and memory.
